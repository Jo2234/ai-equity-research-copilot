BUSINESS OVERVIEW

JPMorgan Chase provides consumer banking, commercial banking, investment banking, asset management, payments, and markets services. The business is sensitive to interest rates, credit quality, capital requirements, client activity, and expense investment.

NET INTEREST INCOME

Net interest income benefited from higher rates and loan balances, partly offset by deposit margin compression and migration to higher-yielding deposit products. Management noted that deposit costs remained a key sensitivity for future net interest income.

CREDIT RISK

The provision for credit losses reflected loan growth and normalization in card delinquencies from unusually low levels. The allowance considered macroeconomic scenarios, unemployment, borrower behavior, commercial real estate exposure, and portfolio seasoning.

CAPITAL MANAGEMENT

The firm's capital position remained above regulatory minimums. Common Equity Tier 1 capital supported loan growth, dividends, share repurchases subject to board approval, and investments in technology and risk management.
